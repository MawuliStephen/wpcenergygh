Ghana’s Membership to the WPC

After a rigorous and competitive applicant selection process for Volunteers from over 30 countries across the world, James Nii Boye Quaye was successfully selected as a Volunteer from Ghana among four other Africa countries and out of over 200 volunteers across the world to participate in the 6th WPC – Youth Forum in St. Petersburg, Russia 2019.

Further search shows that Ghana was not a member of the WPC which necessitated James enquiry with the WPC Secretariat in UK on modalities, requirements, procedures and benefits for a member country for joining the World Petroleum Council (WPC). The Secretariat then arranged to have their Team meet James during the Forum for conclusive guidance for Ghana membership. This development was communicated with the Energy Ministry in 2019 and were happy with the initiative and the Minister, Hon. John-Peter Amewu tasked James to pre-finance the Youth Forum trip and submit a report on the Forum and proposal for Ghana’s membership upon return to the Ministry.

The meeting in Russia with the WPC Secretariat took place on Tuesday, 25th June 2019 at St. Petersburg Mining University’s Gorny Multifunctional Complex between; the Director General of WPC, Dr. Pierce Riemer , the Director of Programme, Sarah Ashmore and James where James was tasked as the WPC contact person (Country Director) for Ghana. In the meeting, James requested for an Official Introductory Letter to Ghana’s Minister of Energy for the membership consideration which came in a letter, dated 18th July 2019 and submitted together with the report and proposal to the Minister as requested on 28th August 2019 with the Ministry’s reference numbers 7325/7326 upon submission. Following which James was tasked a Voluntary Technical Consultant to the Ministry liaising with WPC Secretariat and the Ministry.

There were series of engagements between James and the Petroleum Directorate of the Ministry of Energy on the proposal which resulted in Ghana’s application of intent to join the WPC in the letter dated 19th September, 2019 with reference ZA71/118/01 was sent to the WPC Headquarters. Subsequently Ghana’s membership was approved by the Governing Council of the WPC in their letter dated 4th January 2021
